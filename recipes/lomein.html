Lo Mein

Lo Mein is a popular Chinese noodle dish made with soft wheat noodles, vegetables, and a savory sauce. The noodles are tossed with ingredients such as carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, onions, and protein options like chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu. The sauce is typically made from soy sauce, sesame oil, and other seasonings that give the dish its rich and flavorful taste.

Known for its tender noodles and delicious blend of flavors, Lo Mein is a favorite takeout and homemade meal enjoyed around the world. It is quick to prepare, highly customizable, and can be served as a main dish or as a side alongside other Asian-inspired foods.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces lo mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pound chicken breast, sliced (or beef, shrimp, or tofu)
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 green onions, chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the lo mein noodles according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken and cook until fully cooked and lightly browned.
  4. Add the carrots, bell peppers, cabbage, onion, and garlic. Stir-fry for 3–5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and sugar.
  6. Add the cooked noodles to the skillet and pour the sauce over them.
  7. Toss everything together until the noodles are evenly coated and heated through.
  8. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy.
