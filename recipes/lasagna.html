Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian-inspired baked pasta dish made with layers of wide pasta noodles, rich meat or tomato sauce, creamy cheese, and seasonings. The layers are stacked and baked together until the flavors blend and the cheese becomes melted and golden. Traditional lasagna often includes ground beef or sausage, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, and a tomato-based sauce flavored with garlic, onions, and herbs. Known for its hearty texture and comforting flavor, lasagna is a popular meal for family gatherings and special occasions. The combination of tender pasta, savory sauce, and melted cheese creates a satisfying dish that can be served as a main course alongside salad, garlic bread, or vegetables. Lasagna can also be customized with ingredients such as spinach, mushrooms, or other vegetables, making it a versatile recipe enjoyed by many people around the world.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 small onion
  • 24 ounces of pasta sauce or marinara sauce
  • 15 ounces of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon of italian seasoning
  • 1 large egg
  • salt and black pepper to taste

    Steps

    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Cook the noodles according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
    3. Prepare the meat sauce by cooking the ground beef and onion in a large skillet over medium heat until the beef is browned. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in the pasta sauce and Italian seasoning, then simmer for 10 minutes.
    4. Make the cheese mixture by combining the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the Parmesan cheese in a bowl. Mix well.
    5. Assemble the lasagna in a baking dish:
      • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
      • Add a layer of noodles.
      • Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture.
      • Add a layer of meat sauce.
      • Sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.
      • Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with sauce and mozzarella on top.
    6. Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan cheese over the top.
    7. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    8. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
    9. Serve and enjoy with salad, garlic bread, or your favorite side dish.
